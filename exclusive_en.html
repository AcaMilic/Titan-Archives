Physical arrangement of documentation

Physical arrangement of documentation is the first step in standardization and modernization of business with documentation.

Obligations of legal entities when managing documentation

According to the Law on Cultural Heritage, legal entities are obliged to, in order to manage documentation during their existence:

  1. Mark and date the registration material and keep basic records on it.
  2. Keep the registration material in an orderly and safe condition.
  3. Classify and archive registry material.
  4. Select archival material and extract worthless registry material within one year from the day of expiration of the established storage period.
  5. Determine the manner of recording the registration material, its storage, classification and archiving, unless otherwise provided by law for certain bodies and organizations.
  6. Establish lists of categories of registration material with retention periods.
  7. Determine the manner of protection and use of data and documents created in the process of automatic data processing.

TITAN ARCHIVES offers a service or professional assistance in the physical arrangement of documentation, which includes activities:

  • Review of archival material and registration material in order to determine the entirety of the fund as well as the adoption of a plan and methods of arrangement.
  • Arranging chronologically and by creators.
  • Recording, storage and / or classification when archiving registration material, respecting general and special regulations of obligations for storage of documentary material
  • Formation of the Archive Book.
  • Numbering of archival material and registration material, analogous to the Archive Book.
  • Exclusion of unnecessary registration material whose legal practical need for further storage has expired.

Exclusion implies the separation of registration material whose legal retention period has expired (worthless registration material) from those documentation that are kept permanently (archival material) or whose retention period has not yet expired (documentation that cannot be destroyed yet), and thus establishes up-to-dateness in handling documentation.

The selection of archival material and the extraction of worthless registration material is performed on the basis of the List of categories of registration material with retention periods. However, only from the arranged and adequately listed documentation can the archival material be separated from the worthless registration material.

A special commission in the entity is formed to extract archival material and extract worthless registration material. The Commission lists the worthless registration material in accordance with the established list with retention periods and proposes it for exclusion.
A list of worthless registration material is submitted to the competent archive with a request that the material be reviewed and approved for extraction. Exclusively on the basis of written consent, ie the decision of the competent archive, worthless registration material is destroyed or given for processing as a secondary raw material - old paper.

An expert team of archival workers is working on arranging the documentation.

The process of physical arrangement of documentation is carried out to the level of the archive unit (binder, bundle, folder, file, box).

Editing involves verifying the internal content of the archive unit.
